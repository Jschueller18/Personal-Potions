Understanding Food Servings

For each question below, a "serving" refers to a standard portion of food containing that electrolyte. Click on the blue food name to see examples of what counts as 1 serving.

Sodium

Average American Intake 6.8 servings (3400mg)
Recommended Range 4.6-10 servings (2300-5000mg) Higher for athletes/larger individuals

Sodium-Rich Foods

What counts as 1 serving (500mg):

  • 1/4 tsp salt = 1 serving
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce = 2 servings
  • 3 slices bacon = 1 serving
  • 1 cup canned soup = 1 serving
  • 2 slices of bread = 1/2 serving
  • 2 oz cheese = 1/2 serving
  • 1 frozen meal = 1-2 servings
  • 1 fast food burger = 1 serving

Potassium

Average American Intake 6.25 servings (2500mg)
Recommended Range 8.75-17.5 servings (3500-7000mg) Varies by activity level and size

Potassium-Rich Foods

What counts as 1 serving (400mg):

  • 1 medium banana = 1 serving
  • 1 medium potato = 2 servings
  • 1/2 avocado = 1 serving
  • 1 cup leafy greens = 1 serving
  • 1/2 cup beans = 1 serving
  • 1 cup milk = 1 serving
  • 3 oz salmon = 1 serving
  • 1 cup coconut water = 1 serving

Note: Very high potassium foods (potatoes, avocados) count as 2 servings
